Session 3: Mitigating the Negative Effects of Screen Time

Author: Dr. Aroldo Dargél, MD, Ph.D, Chief Medical & Science Officer, Mindstride

Session 3 Overview

In this session, we will examine the negative effects of excessive screen time and explore strategies to take breaks throughout your day. We will look at how screen time influences your daily habits and impacts your thoughts, feelings, physical activity, and behaviors, potentially leading to negative health outcomes.

Learning Objectives
  1. Identify the Negative Effects of Excessive Screen Time: Understand how too much screen time can impact your sleep, focus, and overall mental and physical health.
  2. Recognize the Importance of Screen Time Breaks: Learn how taking regular breaks from screens can enhance work performance and reduce eye strain.
  3. Increase Awareness of Screen Time's Impact on Overall Health: Continue building your toolkit and knowledge about how screen time and related behaviors can lead to negative health outcomes.
Communication Styles for Managing Irritability

According to the American Psychological Association, adults should limit their daily screen time to 2 hours.

To reduce the negative health effects of excessive screen time, research suggests following the 20-20-20 rule: every twenty minutes, look up from your screen and focus on something approximately twenty feet away for about twenty seconds. This practice can help mitigate eye strain and other negative impacts of too much screen time.

Negative Effects of Excessive Screen Time​

Insomnia and Poor Sleep

  • The light from your device’s screen signals your brain to stay awake.
  • Excessive light exposure throughout the day makes it difficult to unwind at night.

Eye Strain and Headaches

  • Prolonged screen time causes eye fatigue and discomfort.
  • Screen glare and brightness can strain your eyes, leading to headaches or migraines.

Addictive Behaviors

  • Research shows that devices and social media can lead to addictive behaviors, including:
    • Constant thoughts about the device or platform.​
    • Cravings to use the device or platform.
    • Using the device or platform to cope with other situations.
    • Experiencing withdrawal symptoms when unable to access the device or platform.

If the usage of a device or social media platform interferes with your daily life, consider talking to the Mindstride Health Team or seeking help from a health professional.

Neck, Shoulder and Back Pain

  • Typing or holding your phone and looking down strains your neck, shoulders, and back.
  • Holding these positions for long periods can lead to pain and physical health issues.

Cognitive Changes

  • Excessive screen time affects a child’s developing brain.​
  • Adults with smartphone addiction may have impaired brain function, affecting message transmission and overall cognitive performance.

Reduced Physical Activity Levels 

  • Time spent on screens replaces activities like walking, gardening, playing sports, and working on projects.​
  • A sedentary lifestyle is linked to an increased risk of obesity, depression, anxiety, and physical health problems related to muscle and bone health.

Next Steps
