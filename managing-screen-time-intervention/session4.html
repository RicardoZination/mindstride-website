The Impact of Screen Time on Youth Mental Health​

Our devices offer endless opportunities to interact with people and content online, from checking news and emails to scrolling through social media, streaming shows, or video-calling friends. The screen time often exceeds the recommended limit of two hours per day.

Research shows that excessive digital screen use is linked to lower self-reported mental health, including higher levels of depression, perceived stress, and negative moods in adults.

​ It is recommended that adults limit screen time outside of work to less than 2 hours per day. Any additional time should be spent on other activities to reduce or prevent the negative health impacted associated with excessive screen use.

Wrap-up

Technology is evolving rapidly, and it is imperative for you to realize the impact of screen time on your mental and physical health. Limit excessive use of laptops, phones, TVs and other digital devices, to less than two hours per day outside of work, especially before bed, as it can negatively affect your brain, behavior, and general well-being.​​
