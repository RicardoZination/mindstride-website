Session 2: Stress in the Brain

Author: Dr. Aroldo Dargél, MD, Ph.D, Chief Medical & Science Officer, Mindstride

Session Summary

In this second session, we investigate the impact of stress in the brain. We look at the different parts of the brain that are involved when we are experiencing stress. We will also examine the different levels of stress over time and how they impact your thoughts, feelings, and behaviours if stress persists.

As different forms of stress affect your day-to-day activities, we will build your ability to recognize the signs and symptoms of stress, and how to adequately respond to them to prevent exhaustion and burnout.

Learning Objectives
  1. Understand the Role of the Stress in the Brain: Learn that stress is often unavoidable, but you can control your response and its impact on your health.
  2. Recognize the Different Forms of Stress: Identify the signs and symptoms of various types of stress to prevent burnout and other stress-related conditions.
  3. Adopt Habits for Identifying Stress: Build a personalized toolkit to manage stress, improving your health and well-being through better thinking, nutrition, exercise, and social support.
Role of Stress in the Brain
The Spectrum of Stress and Performance

As stress increases, our performance improves up to an optimal point. However, if stress continues to rise, it eventually leads to a suboptimal point where performance and efficiency decline.

Stress Duration:

Acute Stress:Short-term stress occurs when you feel pressured in a specific situation. A certain level of acute stress keeps you alert and focused. However, high levels of acute stress can be unpleasant and reduce performance. This type of stress is best managed with mental or physical stress management techniques, which will be discussed in the

Chronic Stress:Long-term stress happens when stressors accumulate over time. Sustained chronic stress can lead to serious physical and mental health problems if not addressed. Managing chronic stress involves lifestyle changes, adjusting attitudes, and improving your environment.

Different Forms of Stress

Not all stress is bad! Eustress is the good kind of stress that energizes us and boosts creativity. On the other hand, distress can harm self-esteem, cause fatigue, and lead to health issues if it persists for too long.

Recognizing Stress Signs and Symptoms

Recognizing stress signs and symptoms requires patience and self-awareness.

Monitoring your stress-related symptoms across different life stages is crucial for your health and well-being. For instance, a university student may juggle classes, commuting, social life, and student loans, while a government employee might handle commuting, tight deadlines, budgets, and social life.

Both examples involve valid internal and external stressors. It’s important to recognize how our bodies respond to these stressors and build resilience for our well-being.

As you go through this session and learn about the different types of symptoms, remember to consider where you might find yourself on the stress spectrum we discussed earlier.
