Step 1: Filling Out Your Committed Action Journal

(See Journal Example Below)

Every morning, you'll become a Committed Action detective. In your journal, you'll note the times you went to bed and woke up, when you think you fell asleep, and if you woke up during the night, how long you stayed awake. It's like putting together pieces of a puzzle to see the true picture of your sleep.

Record Bedtime and Wake Time: In columns A and B, write the time you went to bed and the time you got up.

Note Committed Action Times: In columns D and E, jot down the time you fell asleep and the time you woke up.

Estimate Awake Time: In column F, estimate the total time you were awake during the night.

Note:  If you have difficulty doing this math, the Mindstride team will help you during your appointment.

Step 2: Calculate Your Average Committed Action Time

Next, we'll use some simple math to uncover the truth behind your sleep. By subtracting the awake times from the total time in bed, we'll calculate how much Committed Action you're really getting. Then, we'll average it out over a week to see your typical Committed Action duration.

Calculate Nightly Committed Action Duration: Subtract the time you were awake during the night from the total time asleep (the difference between the times in columns D and E). Write this total in minutes in column G.

Find Weekly Average: Add all the totals in column G and divide by 7 to find your average Committed Action time per night for the week.

Note:  If you have difficulty doing this math, the Mindstride team will help you during your appointment.

Step 3:  Determine Your Average Time in Bed

Armed with this knowledge, we'll establish your 'Committed Action window'. This is the golden time frame for sleep, tailored just for you, based on your average Committed Action time. If your average Committed Action time is around 5 hours, that's your new bedtime goal.

Calculate Time Spent in Bed: For each night, subtract your bedtime from your wake time to find the total time spent in bed, in minutes, and record this in column C.

Weekly Average Time in Bed: Sum the totals in column C and divide by 7 for your weekly average time in bed per night.

Note:  If you have difficulty doing this math, the Mindstride team will help you during your appointment.

Step 4:  Calculate Your Committed Action Efficiency

Determine Committed Action Efficiency: Divide the number in box H by your total time in bed (box I), multiply by 100 to get your Committed Action efficiency percentage, and record this in box J.

Note:  If you have difficulty doing this math, the Mindstride team will help you during your appointment.

Step 5:  Implement Committed Action Restriction

If your efficiency isn't quite there, we'll adjust your Committed Action window. Think of it as fine-tuning your Committed Action schedule. We might shrink your window slightly to increase the pressure to sleep, helping you fall asleep faster and wake up less during the night.

Remember, this isn't about less sleep; it's about better sleep. With consistent practice and a bit of patience, you'll be on your way to nights filled with restorative sleep and brighter, more energetic days. So, grab your diary, and let's begin this journey to restful nights!

Limit Bed Time: Adjust your time in bed to match your Committed Action window, the average time you spend asleep.

Adjust Based on Efficiency: If your Committed Action efficiency is below 80%, shorten your Committed Action window by 15 minutes, but never below 5 hours. In the example, if the Committed Action window calculates to 5 hours and 6 minutes, and efficiency is low, round down to a 5-hour window.

Fix Your Committed Action Window: Choose a start time for your Committed Action window based on your needs (e.g., if you must wake at 7 AM, your 5-hour window might be from 2 AM to 7 AM). Keep this window consistent daily, even on weekends.

This manual is designed to help you accurately track and improve your sleep habits for better rest and health.
