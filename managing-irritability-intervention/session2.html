Session 2 Overview

In this second session, we will take a further look into your body's response to irritability, including the fight or flight response as well as the most common symptoms that you may be experiencing. Although irritability varies from person-to-person, there are over-arching characteristics that sets it apart from other moods and emotions. We invite you to complete a self-assessment on irritability designed to check-in with your feelings, thoughts, actions and signs in your body. Let's get started.

Learning Objectives
  1. Increase your Understanding About The Signs of Irritability: Learn more about the signs and symptoms your body expresses when you are feeling or about to feel irritated.
  2. Recognize the Other Symptoms Related to Irritability: Increase your awareness of other contributing factors that could be present alongside increased feelings of irritation.
  3. Create a Personalized Toolkit to Improve your Ability to Recognize Irritability: Build on your resources by completing a self-assessment sheet designed to identify thoughts, feelings, actions, and signs in your body that could indicate irritability.
Understanding Your Body's Response to Irritability

You may experience physical sensations when your irritability levels increase. This is your body’s way of signaling that you are reacting to stress, fear, or anxiety, indicating a real or perceived threat.

This response is commonly known as the 'fight or flight' response, which prepares you to protect yourself or escape the situation.

Seeking Help for Irritability

Irritability is not considered a mental health condition and therefore cannot be formally diagnosed. However, people experiencing irritability should still seek help when needed. Seeking mental health care to manage feelings of irritability is very common.

Different psychological interventions can help you understand the root cause of your irritability and teach you novel coping mechanisms to better understand and manage your emotions. At your next check-in with your Mindstride Team, discuss with them some alternatives to better manage feelings of irritability.
