Session 4: Reducing Irritability

Session 4 Overview

In this fourth and final session, we will identify strategies that could help reduce irritability and increase your mental health, such as physical exercise and other lifestyle factors. As we have learned, when we are experiencing irritability we can draw from our own skillsets to help you plan and navigate difficult situations. This session further helps you identify the strengths you can rely on to improve your mental health and implement healthy behaviours to see their benefits in your day-to-day activities.​

Learning Objectives
  1. Increase Your Awareness of the Resources to Manage Irritability: Utilizing your existing tools and resources, you will identify your strengths in how you can cope and reduce irritability.
  2. Recognize the Role of Your Lifestyle Habits  in your Mental Health: Increase your awareness of how your day-to-day activities and interactions can be helpful in reducing irritability.
  3. Build on your Personalized Toolkit to Improve your Ability to Reduce Irritability: Build on your resources and skills by making informed, healthy lifestyle choices and build habits reducing irritability and expressing your feelings in a positive, healthy way.
Strategies to Reduce Irritability and Enhance Your Mental Health
A. Identifying Your Skills and Resources

Take a moment to reflect on the people who support you, such as family, friends, relatives, colleagues, and loved ones. Have you reached out to them when you're feeling irritated?

Consider your confidence in coping with irritability. Can you work out solutions or strategies during stressful situations? As you read through these sessions, have you noticed if irritability has affected your thoughts, feelings, and actions? Are you taking time for yourself to navigate feelings of irritation?

Review the checkboxes below and mark the skills and resources you have used or plan to use to reduce irritability in your daily life.

B. Taking Time for Yourself

If you are feeling irritated, it's important to take time for yourself. This helps you reflect, process, and reduce irritation.

Restart your day with activities you enjoy to improve your mood and overall mental health. These activities can calm your body and mind, help you sleep better, and leave you feeling well-rested. Without taking time to unwind, it's easy to feel overwhelmed and stressed.

Choose an activity to look forward to at the end of the day or week that gives you a break and shifts your mindset to a positive note.​

C. Implementing Healthy Behaviors

The important thing is that you are making strides to control and improve your mental well-being.
