How Does Burnout Affect Our Mental Health?

Burnout can affect your mental health and brain health including your brain’s cognitive functioning. Your experiences of burnout may be disrupting your attention, creativity, problem solving ability, short-term memory as well as your ability to regulate emotions properly.​

The Link Between Burnout and Mental Health Conditions​

Burnout and mental health conditions are interconnected, creating a cycle where one can lead to the other. Research shows that burnout can lead to conditions like depression, anxiety, insomnia, and alcohol dependence. It's crucial to monitor your health and wellness when you experience high levels of stress at work or in your personal life.​​

The connection between work stress and health problems can be:

  • Direct: Stress can trigger or accelerate the development of illnesses.
  • Indirect:Stress can lead to negative health behaviors associated with mental health conditions.
  • Reversed: Coping with a physical or mental health condition can increase stress levels, potentially leading to burnout.

Pay attention to your stress levels and take proactive steps to manage them for better overall health and well-being.
