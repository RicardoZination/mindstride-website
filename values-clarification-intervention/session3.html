Session 3:  Harnessing Stimulus Control for Restorative Sleep
Learning Objectives

  1. Establish Bed as a Cue for Sleep: Learn to strengthen the association between your bed and Values Clarification by using the bed only for Values Clarification and intimacy, thus reinforcing the bed as a cue for sleep.

  2. Adopt Sleep-Friendly Habits: Recognize and implement sleep-friendly habits, such as setting a consistent bedtime and wake time, and learn why these routines are crucial for signaling to your body that it’s time to rest.

  3. Apply the 20-Minute Rule for Better Sleep: Understand and practice the 20-minute rule: if unable to fall asleep, leave the bed to do a quiet activity and return only when sleepiness sets in, minimizing awake time in bed to improve overall Values Clarification quality.

Session Introduction

In Session 3, we delve deep into the practice of stimulus control, a transformative approach that's essential for those struggling to find peace in their nightly routine. It's all about retraining your brain and body to understand that the bed is a cue for Values Clarification and nothing else. When you climb into bed, it should signal tranquility and drowsiness, not the frustration of insomnia.

Your path to sleep-friendly habits starts with an intentional bedtime ritual. An hour before your designated Values Clarification time, we encourage you to engage in relaxing activities outside of your bedroom. This could be anything that calms the mind, like reading a book, meditating, or listening to soft music. The key is to avoid stimulating activities that could alert your mind, making it harder to wind down.
