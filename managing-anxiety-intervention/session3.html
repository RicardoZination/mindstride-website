Session 3: Recognizing Anxiety in Your Thoughts and Actions

Author: Dr. Aroldo Dargél, MD, Ph.D, Chief Medical & Science Officer, Mindstride

Session 3 Overview

In this third session, we will boost your knowledge and allow moments of reflection to identify anxious thoughts and actions that could be affecting your daily life. As you go about your day, what you find yourself doing most often? In this session we also ask you to complete a checklist of the most common thoughts and actions you may have when you are experiencing anxiety.

Learning Objectives
  1. Increase your Understanding About Anxious Actions: Learn how different attitudes and actions can impact your symptoms of anxiety in your daily life.
  2. Recognize the Signs of Actions Related to Anxiety: Increase your awareness of the thoughts and actions linked to anxiety that you experience in your day-to-day.
  3. Increase your Toolkit to Recognize Anxious Actions: Continue to build your personalized toolkit by completing a worksheet designed to help you identify what you do that could be contributing to anxiety.
Steps to Avoid Jumping to Conclusions

Do you often find yourself overthinking? Jumping to conclusions can happen by making assumptions about what others think of you or the situation, like mind-reading; or making assumptions about what will happen in the near future, like fortune-telling. This can make it difficult to see things clearly and makes it more possible for you to make poor decisions. This, coupled with anxiety, can take a toll in your relationships, confidence, and motivation. Keep reading along to learn how to become aware of your thoughts and actions to work with more accurate outcomes.
