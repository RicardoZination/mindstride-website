Your guide to understand anxiety

What is anxiety?

Anxiety is a thought or feeling that makes you feel uneasy or uncomfortable.

Is anxiety an important emotion?

Yes, anxiety is an important emotion that helps protect us from harm.

Can anxiety affect anyone?

Yes, anxiety can affect anyone at any stage of life, regardless of age, gender, income, or personality type. If you're feeling anxious, you're not alone.​

Is anxiety all good or all bad?

It's not that simple. Some anxiety can be beneficial, motivating us to get to work on time, meet deadlines, explore new places, and stay aware of our surroundings. However, too much anxiety can disrupt your health and daily activities. It depends on how often and how intense your anxiety is..

Why is it important to share stories about anxiety?

Sharing your stories about anxiety and how you've dealt with it can help others and make us all feel less alone.

Causes of Anxiety

There are several factors that can cause anxiety, and they can be different for each person. Research shows that these factors can be biological, psychological, and social. This means:

  • Biological factors: These are things you are born with, like your genetics and brain chemistry.
  • Psychological factors:These are related to your lifestyle, such as your thoughts, behaviors, and ways of coping with stress.
  • Social factors:These involve your life experiences and early learning, including your relationships, environment, and events you've been through.

Everyone's experience with anxiety is unique because these factors combine in different ways for each person.

The Fight or Flight Response

When we face immediate stress, our body's automatic survival mechanism kicks in to deal with the threat or danger, whether it's real or imagined. This is known as the fight or flight response. During this response, the body makes several changes to prepare us to either confront the threat or run away from it. While anxiety can feel intense, it's not harmful by itself; it simply puts the body in a state of alertness to handle the situation.

What is happening in the body?

When our body goes into fight or flight mode, our mind signals that there's immediate danger. The main purpose of the fight or flight response is to protect us. When this response is activated, three major systems are affected:

  • Physical (Body): Your body undergoes changes like increased heart rate and muscle tension.
  • Cognitive (Thinking):Your thoughts become focused on the danger and how to respond to it.
  • Behavioral (Actions): You either prepare to confront the threat or run away from it. Sometimes, people may also experience a freeze response. This happens when the body is in shock or facing extreme stress, causing you to feel stuck and unable to move.
