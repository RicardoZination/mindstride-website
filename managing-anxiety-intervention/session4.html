Session 4: Preventing and Reducing Anxiety

Session 4 Overview

In this fourth and final session we will explore your skills and strengths that can help you navigate anxious thoughts and behaviours. Each of us has protective factors that could reduce anxiety and even help you prevent anxious thoughts from persisting in your mind. In this session you can identify those characteristics while also learning new strategies to reduce the impact of anxiety in your day-to-day activities, such as task management and breathing exercises.

Learning Objectives
  1. Increase your Knowledge About Managing Anxiety: Discover what skills and resources you have within your reach to help manage and reduce anxiety.
  2. Recognize the Signs of Worrying Thoughts: Increase your awareness of the negative thoughts and actions linked to anxiety that come to mind when you are undergoing a stressful situation.
  3. Expand your Toolkit to Reduce Anxiety: Continue to build your toolkit by adding practices and actions to take when you are experiencing anxiety.
Challenging Your Thoughts

The Mindstride Team has curated a list of examples for your personal toolkit that can be used when you’re dealing with anxious thoughts. Review the list below and feel free to add more items as you progress through this intervention.

Shifting Your Beliefs About Worry

A stressful event or situation in your life can bring you worrying thoughts about what we are to expect next. It is important to reflect on your initial thoughts and catch the negative mindset that may go in circles in your head. Your worries are valid, and it also important to think about the big picture and see what the real possibilities of outcomes are. Here are some suggestions to reduce and manage worrying thoughts:

5 Ways to Reduce the Impact of Anxiety
  1. 1. Stop Procrastinating
    Control what you can. Simplify or reduce your tasks to lessen stress.
  2. 2. Delegate Tasks
    Hand off tasks that others can do or seek help. It’s okay to say no if you’re overwhelmed.
  3. 3. Take Notes
    Write things down to organize your thoughts and provide clarity.
  4. 4. Spend Time with Friends and Family
    Be social! Loved ones can support you through stressful times. Share your experiences with them.
  5. 5. Practice Breathing Exercises
    Breathe in, breathe out. Focus on slow, deep breaths. This helps you feel calmer and more relaxed..
Wrap-up

Anxiety may play a major role in your life, and managing anxious thoughts and actions takes time. Using the skills, resources, and life experiences in your toolbox will help you manage anxiety and its effects on your daily activities.

Remember, there may be days when you feel more anxious than usual—and that’s okay! The important thing is that you are making strides to identify and reduce it as it comes.

We hope you see continued improvement as you set more goals to manage anxiety.
