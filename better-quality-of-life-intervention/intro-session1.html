Session 1: Understanding Quality of Life

Session 1 Summary​

In this first session, we'll define what quality of life means and dive into its various components, including physical and social health. We'll explore the key characteristics that contribute to quality of life and how they impact your mental health and well-being. Additionally, we'll discuss how different aspects of mental health influence your personal perception of quality of life.

Learning Objectives

  1. Increase your Understanding of Quality of Life: Discover how your life dimensions (e.g., social, physical, mental, environmental) influence your daily life.
  2. Recognize the Role of Mental Health in Quality of Life: Understand how your mental health impacts your perception of quality of life and affects your daily functioning.
  3. Identify the Main Characteristics of Quality of Life: Expand your knowledge about the key domains that shape your quality of life and learn how to enhance these areas.

Causes of Anxiety

We often hear the term "quality of life," but what does it mean for you?

Do you believe you have a good quality of life?

What is your viewpoint on quality of life?

Answering these questions may take more than just one sentence because quality of life is quite complex. It's highly subjective, meaning it's based on your own perception and circumstances.

The World Health Organization World Health Organization defines quality of life as an individual's perception of their position in life in the context of the culture and value systems in which they live, and in relation to their goals, expectations, standards, and concerns.

Quality of Life and Mental Health​

Mental health, like any other facet of quality of life, plays a crucial role in shaping a person's perception of their quality of life. For instance, someone living with anxiety or depression may find their symptoms disrupt daily activities or make tasks feel overwhelming. Your mental health can either support or hinder your ability to complete daily tasks.

Those with a mental illness or who experience symptoms know that some days are manageable while others require stepping back to handle symptoms. ​

This journey offers a unique and valid perspective on quality of life.

Understanding the Key Domains of Quality of Life​

You can also think of your quality of life as the degree to which you enjoy the important possibilities of life. These possibilities arise from the opportunities and limitations each person faces, reflecting the interaction between personal and environmental factors. For example, if you live in a home with a lot of green space, you are more likely to use your outdoor areas compared to someone living in a densely populated urban area.
