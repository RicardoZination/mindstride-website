Session 2: Identifying Quality of Life

Author: Dr. Aroldo Dargél, MD, Ph.D, Chief Medical & Science Officer, Mindstride

Session 2 Overview

In this second session, we will explore the different factors that make you who you are, and how these factors ultimately determine your perception of quality of life. The quality-of-life model presented in this session will give you an overview of some circumstances you have to consider when self-assessing your quality of life on a day-to-day basis.​​

Learning Objectives
  1. Increase your Knowledge About Quality of Life: Understand the factors that contribute to your overall quality of life and identify the people who support your daily activities.
  2. Identify the Domains in Your Quality of Life: Reflect on the factors and provide examples from your daily activities that impact your quality of life.
  3. Build your Awareness of the Complexity of Quality of Life: Deepen your understanding of the various components that shape your perception of quality of life.
Quality of Life Model​

Think about your daily routine. Do your family, friends, or colleagues have similar lifestyles?​

Sometimes they might, but it's crucial to remember that everyone experiences life differently. Below, we propose a model to explore how various circumstances can affect your quality of life.

The input from your culture, demographic (age, sex, gender, etc.), and your socioeconomic conditions receive influence from your family, friends, work, home life, community, health, education, and spirituality. In other words, the factors that make you you, from your background, your heritage, how old you are, who you see on a regular basis, and the level of your income, are all connected to your own person; then there are people (family, friends, colleagues), places (work, community) ,and other factors (your health, spirituality, and education) that give you opinions and information on how you view your quality of life. In looking at the chart above from left to right, all of these inputs come together to determine your quality of life.

As you continue with these session, reflect on the following questions:

  • Which of these has the most influence in your life?
  • How do you feel about each of these inputs?
  • Is there one influence that you would like to work on the most?
