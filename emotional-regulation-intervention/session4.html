Session 4:  Navigating  Difficult Emotions

Session 4 Overview

In this fourth session, we will continue to build your knowledge and awareness about how you can be better at navigating difficult situations. We will look into the different circumstances around men and women impact the ability to express emotions as well as how the cycle of distress can prolong negative feelings and stressful situations. Additionally, you will also complete a questionnaire to reflect on your experiences with emotions.

Learning Objectives
  1. Increase your Understanding About Emotional Regulation Across Lifespan: Learn how emotions are a normal part of everyday life and how your ability to respond to emotional experiences can vary from person to person and change over the years.
  2. Recognize the Role of Emotions and Behaviors in your Mental Health: Increase your awareness of the biological, social, cultural, and experiential factors that influence how you process and express emotions.
  3. Identify and Discuss Your Previous Experiences in Regulating Emotions: Build on your reflective practices by completing a worksheet designed to highlight your experiences and strengths in navigating positive and negative events.
Navigating Strong Emotions – What are they? Why do they happen?

Managing difficult thoughts and emotions isn't a one-size-fits-all solution. It depends on each individual and their life circumstances. What works for you might not work for someone else. The key is to build your knowledge and tools to improve your mental health and well-being.

Gender and Emotional Expression

Men and women may experience the same emotions but process and express them differently, often due to societal expectations and cultural differences. For example, men in some cultures are taught not to express their emotions, while women are encouraged to talk about their feelings. These differences can also vary from household to household. Adolescents can find it challenging to regulate their emotions due to growth and developmental changes. The intensity of emotions can shift throughout our lives and even from day to day.

The Distress Cycle

When strong emotions arise, you might feel the urge to reduce or act upon them immediately. This can lead to strong negative feelings, making it difficult to manage the emotions effectively. When you seek to avoid or reduce the experience of a negative emotion, you enter what is called the distress cycle.

The Distress Cycle

his cycle describes the common "build-up phase" where you experience an unpleasant event that triggers negative feelings in your body and mind. Examples include a difficult discussion with your superiors or being late to an important meeting with your coworkers.​
