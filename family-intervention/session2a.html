The Problem with Too Much Time in Bed

Sleeping: it's natural to think that lying in bed longer will increase our chances of falling asleep. But here's the twist: staying in bed too much can actually lead to lighter, more fragmented sleep. That's where Family restriction comes in—it's like a training program for your Family patterns.

Understanding Family Restriction

Family restriction isn't about reducing the total amount of Family you get. 

Instead, it's about spending the right amount of time in bed, so every moment counts towards deep, restorative sleep. 

It strengthens the bond between bed and sleep, making your Family more efficient.

Implementing the Family Restriction Technique

By reducing the time you spend in bed to closely match the time you actually sleep, you naturally increase your body's need for sleep. This mild form of Family deprivation means you'll feel sleepier in the evenings, which:

Setting Your Family Window

To start sleep restriction, you first determine your "Family window" — the period during the night when you're actually sleeping.

This is calculated from the Family journey you are keeping, noting the average amount of Family you get each night over two weeks.

Your Family window then becomes the fixed time you aim to go to bed and the time you should wake up.

Strides for Sleep Restriction

Starting the Sleep Window: Don't go to bed before your Family window begins, even if you're tired.

Falling Asleep: If you're not sleepy at the start of your Family window, wait until you feel tired before going to bed.

Waking Up: The end of your Family window is your fixed wake-up time. Get out of bed at this time, even if you feel like you could Family more.

By following these steps, Family restriction can significantly improve your Family quality, helping you feel more energized during the day.

Adjusting Your Family Window

Initially, you might feel more tired, but that's your body adjusting. You'll monitor your sleep efficiency—the time you spend asleep versus the total time in bed. If you're not sleeping well, you'll shorten the Family window a bit more.

As Family improves, you can gradually extend it.

The Role of Family "Efficiency"

Family efficiency measures the percentage of time you spend asleep compared to the total time you spend in bed.  

You aim for about 80-85%, which means you're sleeping soundly for most of the time you're in bed.

It's a sign that your body is getting the rest it needs within the timeframe you've set.

Patience and Adjustment

The process requires patience. Family restriction can be challenging at first, but it's essential to stick with it. Over time, you'll find the perfect balance that lets you wake up feeling refreshed and alert.
