Main Characteristics of Interpersonal Skills

Interpersonal skills not only benefit your mental health but also enhance the overall well-being of those around you. By surrounding yourself with people from various backgrounds and showing understanding, empathy, concern, and politeness, you demonstrate great interpersonal skills. Here are the main characteristics of a person with strong interpersonal skills:​

  • Empathy: The ability to understand and share the feelings of others.
  • Active Listening: Fully focusing on the speaker, understanding their message, and responding thoughtfully.​
  • Clear Communication: Expressing ideas and feelings clearly and effectively.
  • Politeness: Demonstrating respect and consideration for others.
  • Adaptability: Being flexible and open to different perspectives and situations.​
  • Positive Attitude: Maintaining an optimistic and supportive demeanor.​​
  • Collaboration: Working well with others to achieve common goals.​
  • Conflict Resolution: Effectively managing and resolving disagreements.​
Enhancing Mental Health Through Strong Interpersonal Skills and Healthy Workplace Culture​

Your interpersonal skills along with your workplace culture play a major role in your mental health and wellness. Your personal and professional life’s stressors can contribute to negative mental health outcomes when they are present for a long period of time. Through the factors below, you along with your workplace can promote and improve employee mental health:

  • Supportive Environment: Foster a culture of support and understanding.
  • Effective Communication: Encourage open and honest dialogue.​
  • Work-Life Balance: Promote policies that help balance personal and professional responsibilities.​
  • Recognition and Appreciation: Acknowledge and value employees' contributions.​
  • Stress Management Resources: Provide tools and resources to manage stress effectively.​
  • Professional Development: Offer opportunities for growth and learning.​​
  • Empathy and Compassion: Cultivate a workplace where empathy and compassion are practiced regularly.​

On the other hand, studies have shown that an unfavorable workplace—characterized by a lack of social support, poor work-life balance, excessive job demands, and insufficient policies—can negatively impact employee mental health and well-being.

Types of Communication Skills​

In the previous session, we explored four basic communication skills. In this session, we will build on your effective communication skills by focusing on three main types of communication skills: assertive communication, conversational communication, and non-verbal communication.

Each of these skills will enhance your interpersonal abilities by promoting open, clear, and active communication with others.

Assertive Communication

An assertive style of communication balances your needs with those of others. It involves being accommodating while setting necessary boundaries. In this approach, your attitude and expression should be calm, confident, and considerate.​​

Conversational Communication​

A conversational style of communication involves the ability to exchange ideas, information, and feedback, and to work collaboratively to solve problems. In the workplace, effective conversational communication enhances productivity, boosts employee satisfaction, and contributes to long-term success.

Non-Verbal Communication

Non-verbal communication plays a significant role in building interpersonal skills. It encompasses everything we convey with our bodies, such as eye contact, tone of voice, and body posture. These non-verbal cues can send powerful messages to those around us, regardless of what we say verbally. Therefore, it's crucial to be mindful of both our words and our actions.

When you are about to talk to someone, take a quick second to think about the following:

  • How are you feeling?
  • How interested are you in the conversation or situation?
  • How much do you know about the specific topic being discussed?​
  • What is your stance or view on the topic?​
  • Are you being open and honest about what you are communicating?​

Reflecting on these questions can help you engage more effectively and authentically.
