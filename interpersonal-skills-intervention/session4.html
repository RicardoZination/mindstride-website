Session 4: Strategies to Improve Interpersonal Skills

Author: Dr. Aroldo Dargél, MD, Ph.D, Chief Medical & Science Officer, Mindstride

Session 4 Overview

In this fourth and final session, we will explore tips to improve your interpersonal skills, focusing on effective communication, emotional intelligence, and adaptability, among others. As we go through this session, we will also highlight some scientific evidence on the mental health benefits of building your interpersonal skills in the workplace.​​

Learning Objectives
  1. Increase Your Knowledge on Applying Interpersonal Skills: Learn effective strategies and qualities to implement interpersonal skills in your daily life.
  2. Recognize the Impact of Interpersonal Skills on your Mental Health: Understand how your mental health is influenced by the interpersonal skills and relationships you build in the workplace.
  3. ​Create a Personalized Toolkit to Improve your Interpersonal Skills: Develop your resources and skills by making informed decisions on how to practice your interpersonal skills using the tools and insights from these sessions.
Tips for Improving Interpersonal Skills​

Valued interpersonal relationships, whether in your family dynamics, friendships, or workplace, ease frustration and its related anxiety and stress. For example, your increased work interpersonal skills, along with its relationships, help reduce turnover, improve performance, and boost your overall mental health. Health Benefits to Building Interpersonal Skills at Work​
Wrap-up

Building effective interpersonal skills through communication, collaboration, and empathy can significantly improve your relationships and contribute to your mental health and well-being.​

While encountering new people and situations, keep in mind those around you and yourself. Foster a mindset of openness, honesty, and active listening, and communicate your ideas clearly. Pay close attention to your verbal and non-verbal signals.

The key is that you are making progress towards enhancing your mental well-being.

We hope you continue to notice improvements as you aim to further develop your interpersonal skills.​

Next Steps

Evidence points to the health benefits linked to effective interpersonal skills such as reduced stress, anxiety, depression, and burnout. With this knowledge, the tips provided in this session lead to strides to improving your interpersonal skills.
